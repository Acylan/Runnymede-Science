CanSat

A real engineering challenge: fit an entire working satellite inside a drinks can, launch it, and have it run a mission as it parachutes back to Earth.

🥤 What is a CanSat?

A CanSat is a tiny satellite that must fit the size and shape of a standard 330 ml drinks can — about 115 mm tall and 66 mm across. Inside that space the team packs a power supply, a microcontroller, sensors, and a radio. A rocket or drone carries it up to roughly 1 km, releases it, and the CanSat must survive the fall, collect data, and send it back. It's the smallest way to experience the full life cycle of a space mission: design, build, test, launch, and analyse.

🎯 The mission

📡

Primary mission

Measure air temperature and pressure at least once per second during the descent and transmit the readings live to the ground station as telemetry.

🪂

Secondary mission

Your team's own challenge — for example a controlled descent (parachute or auto-gyro), GPS tracking, or landing accuracy. This is where creativity earns marks.

🧑‍🚀 Team roles

🔧

Structure

Designs the chassis and recovery system so everything fits and survives landing.

⚡

Electronics

Wires up the sensors, microcontroller, battery and radio.

💻

Software

Programs the flight code and the ground-station software that logs the data.

📊

Data & Comms

Analyses the telemetry, makes the graphs and presents the results.

🗓️ How a season runs

1 · Design review

Plan the mission, sketch the design and prove it can fit and work on paper.

2 · Build & integrate

Assemble the electronics, write the code and pack it all into the can.

3 · Test

Drop tests, range tests and a full dress rehearsal of the telemetry link.

4 · Launch day

The CanSat flies, runs its mission and streams data to the ground station.

5 · Report

Analyse the results and present what you learned.

Want to join the CanSat team?

No experience needed — there's a role for every interest, from soldering and coding to design and presenting. Speak to the STEM coordinator to sign up.
